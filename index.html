About Me


Doctor - Physiologist - Neuroscientist - Carnatic vocalist
Here is my quick Introduction: I am a medical doctor from India. As an Assistant Professor in a Medical College in India (2010-2021) I completed 3 funded projects, with over 20 publications. In this position, I taught different medical and paramedical courses (MBBS, MD, BDS, MDS, BPT, B.E Medical Electronics, B.Sc Nursing, overall >400 students/year). I am a keen academician with a lot of interest in disseminating scientific knowledge with colleagues and students [consistently rated >4.5 out of 5 by my students, 2010-2021].  


I am currently a postdoc in Thierry Charlier's lab, in France studying the effect of anthropogenic stimulants, including toxins on neuroplasticity and behavior using zebrafish and mouse models. For this, I use molecular biology approaches.

Research (2005-2021)


My First Research Experience (2005-2006):
During my bachelor in Medicine, (residency training), I worked on one clinical trial and project studying the effect of meteorological fluctuation on asthma attacks in the emergency room, under the supervision of Dr. B V MuraliMohan (MRCP, UK).
Through data collection and analysis of 13 hospitals across the city, we identified that the average temperature, particulate matter (PM), and nitrogen dioxide play a major role in acute asthma admission, with PM and NO2 having a lag effect for 5 days.
This research was selected for an oral presentation at the National Pulmonologists Conference (NAPCON 2005) and was published later on.
Check out the Publication!  


MD Thesis (2007-2010):
In my experience of working as a shift worker (2005-2008), the stress involved in hospital settings during the night times was immense.
As a fresh MD student, I conceptualized the idea of probably impaired cardiovascular responses in shift workers and early detection of the same for my MD thesis.
I showed that insomnia for just 1 week in shift workers led to a reduction in heart rate variability (HRV), which is a precursor risk for future heart diseases
For this work, I won the Prof. N. Padmanabhan Memorial Award - best paper by any Postgraduate student in Cardiovascular Physiology – Instituted by Indian association of biomedical sciences (IABMS),India.
Check out the Publication!  


Research (2010-2021): My lab was named the Cognition and Music Perception (CAMP) Lab
Visit CAMP Lab Webpage!
I completed 3 funded projects (as Principal Investigator) - ~65k€
Funded by Indian Council of Medical Research and Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences.
The research broadly covered preventive strategies for non-communicable disorders.
I studied the effect of lifestyle modification versus added acoustic stimulation using Indian music to reduce hypertension/prehypertension among patients.
Later I studied over 400 healthy individuals for neuro and cardiovascular effects of acoustic stimulation with different melodic scales of music.
For this, I included biomarkers of stress and anxiety scales along with heart rate variability and electroencephalography as parameters.
